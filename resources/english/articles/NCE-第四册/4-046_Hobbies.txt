a gifted American psychologist has said, 'Worry is a spasm of the emotion; the mind catches hold of something and will not let it go.' It is useless to argue with the mind in this condition. ->> 一位天才的美国心理学家曾经说过：“烦恼是感情的发作，脑子会纠缠住某种东西不肯松手。”在这种情况下，与头脑争执是无济于事的。

The stronger the will, the more futile the task. ->> 意志越强烈，尝试就越显得徒劳。

One can only gently insinuate something else into its convulsive grasp. ->> 只能委婉地让另一种事物介入这种痉挛的掌控中。

And if this something else is rightly chosen, if it really attended by the illumination of another field of interest, gradually, and often quite swiftly, the old undue grip relaxes and the process of recuperation and repair begins. ->> 如果选择得当，并且的确受到另一个领域的启发，那么过去不当的紧张就会逐渐甚至迅速松弛，恢复和修整的过程就会开始。

The cultivation of a hobby and new forms of interest is therefore a policy of the first importance to a public man. ->> 因此，对一个公众人物来说，培养一种业余爱好和新的兴趣形式是非常重要的策略。

But this is not a business that can be undertaken in a day or swiftly improvised by a mere command of the will. ->> 但这并不是能够一日之功，或仅仅凭借意志的命令就能迅速完成的事情。

The growth of alternative mental interests is a long process. ->> 培养多种精神兴趣是一个漫长的过程。

The seeds must be carefully chosen; they must fall on good ground; they must be sedulously tended, if the vivifying fruits are to be at hand when needed. ->> 要想在需要时获得充满生气的果实，就必须精心选择种子，把它们种在肥沃的土地上，并勤奋地护理。

To be really happy and really safe, one ought to have at least two or three hobbies, and they must all be real. ->> 要真正感到幸福和安全，一个人应该至少有两三个爱好，而且必须是真实的。

It is no use starting late in life to say: 'I will take an interest in this or that.' Such an attempt only aggravates the strain of mental effort. ->> 到了晚年才开始说：“我会对这个或那个感兴趣”是没有用的，这种尝试只会加剧精神压力。

A man may acquire great knowledge of topics unconnected with his daily work, and yet get hardly any benefit or relief. ->> 一个人可能会获得与日常工作无关的话题的丰富知识，但几乎得不到什么益处或宽慰。

It is no use doing what you like; you have got to like what you do. ->> 干自己喜欢的事是没有用的，必须要喜欢你所做的事。

Broadly speaking, human beings may be divided into three classes: those who are toiled to death, those who are worried to death, and those who are bored to death. ->> 泛泛地说，人类可以分为三类：劳累至死的人、忧虑至死的人和无聊至死的人。

It is no use inviting the politician or the professional or business man, who has been working or worrying about serious things for six days, to work or worry about trifling things at the weekend. ->> 没有必要邀请一位忙于或忧虑重要事务六天的政客、专业人士或商人在周末为琐事工作或担忧。

As for the unfortunate people who can command everything they want, who can gratify every caprice and lay their hands on almost every object of desire -- for them a new pleasure, a new excitement if only an additional satiation. ->> 至于那些能任意支配一切、能满足每一个异想天开并几乎能够获得他们想要的任何东西的不幸者来说，新的快乐和刺激不过是额外的满足。

In vain they rush frantically round from place to place, trying to escape from avenging boredom by mere clatter and motion. ->> 他们徒劳地到处狂奔，试图通过喧闹和活动逃避无聊的惩罚。

For them discipline in one form or another is the most hopeful path. ->> 对他们来说，以某种形式进行自律是一条最有希望的道路。

It may also be said that rational, industrious, useful human beings are divided into two classes. ->> 也可以说，理智的、勤劳的、有用的人可以分为两类。

Of these the former are the majority. ->> 在这两类人中，前者占多数。

They have their compensations. ->> 他们有自己的补偿。

The long hours in the office or the factory bring with them as their reward, not only the means of sustenance, but a keen appetite for pleasure even in its simplest and most modest forms. ->> 在办公室或工厂的长时间工作不仅带来了谋生手段， 还激发了对简单和朴实的乐趣的强烈渴望。

But Fortune's favoured children belong to the second class. ->> 然而，命运之神的宠儿属于第二类。

Their life is a natural harmony. ->> 他们的生活是自然和谐的。

For them the working hours are never long enough. ->> 对他们来说，工作时间永远不够长。

Each day is a holiday, and ordinary holidays, when they come, are grudged as enforced interruptions in an absorbing vocation. ->> 每天都是假期，而普通的假期，当它们来临时，被视为强制中断专心工作的不便之事。

Yet to both classes, the need of an alternative outlook, of a change of atmosphere, of a diversion of effort, is essential. ->> 然而， 对这两类人来说，需要另一种视角、换个氛围、分散精力是必须的。

Indeed, it may well be that those whose work is their pleasure are those who most need the means of banishing it at intervals from their minds. ->> 事实上，工作是乐趣的人可能更需要定期将其从头脑中排除的方法。
