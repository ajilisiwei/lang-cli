Several cases have been reported in Russia recently of people who can detect colours with their fingers, and even see through solid walls. ->> 俄罗斯最近报导了几起事例，有人能用手指辨认颜色，甚至能透过坚实的墙壁看到东西。
One case concerns an eleven-year-old schoolgirl, Vera Petrova, who has normal vision but who can also perceive things with different parts of her skin, and through solid walls. ->> 其中一例涉及一个11岁的女孩，维拉·彼托洛娃，她的视力与常人一样，但还能用皮肤的不同部位察觉事物，甚至透过坚实的墙壁。
This ability was first noticed by her father. ->> 是她父亲首先发现她这一能力的。
One day she came into his office and happened to put her hands on the door of a locked safe. ->> 一天，维拉走进父亲的办公室，偶然把手放在一个锁着的保险柜的门上。
Suddenly she asked her father why he kept so many old newspapers locked away there, and even described the way they were done up in bundles. ->> 她突然问父亲为什么把这么多旧报纸锁在柜子里，并且描述了报纸捆扎的情况。
Vera's curious talent was brought to the notice of a scientific research institute in the town of Ulyanovsk, near where she lives, and in April she was given a series of tests by a special commission of the Ministry of Health of the Russian Federal Republic. ->> 维拉的特异功能引起了她家附近的乌里扬诺夫斯克城一个科研单位的注意。4月里，俄罗斯联邦卫生部的一个特别委员会对她进行了一系列的测试。
During these tests she was able to read a newspaper through an opaque screen and, stranger still, by moving her elbow over a child's game of Lotto she was able to describe the figures and colours printed on it; and, in another instance, wearing stockings and slippers, to make out with her foot the outlines and colours of a picture hidden under a carpet. ->> 在这些测试中，她能隔着不透明的屏幕读报纸。更奇怪的是，她通过移动肘部在儿童玩的“罗托”纸牌上能说出印在纸牌上的数字和颜色；还有一次，她穿着长筒袜和拖鞋，用脚能识别出藏在地毯下的一幅画的轮廓和颜色。

During all these tests Vera was blindfold; and, indeed, except when blindfold she lacked the ability to perceive things with her skin. ->> 在所有这些测试中，维拉的双眼都是蒙着的。事实上，如果不蒙住双眼，她的皮肤就不再具有识别事物的能力。
