Custom has not commonly been regarded as a subject of great moment. ->> 风俗一般未被认为是什么重要的课题。
The inner workings of our own brains we feel to be uniquely worthy of investigation, but custom, we have a way of thinking, is behaviour at its most commonplace. ->> 我们觉得，只有我们大脑内部的活动情况才值得研究，而风俗呢，只是些司空见惯的行为而已。
As a matter of fact, it is the other way around. ->> 事实上，情况正好相反。
Traditional custom, taken the world over, is a mass of detailed behaviour more astonishing than what any one person can ever evolve in individual actions, no matter how aberrant. ->> 从世界范围来看，传统风俗是由许多细节性的习惯行为组成的，比任何一个人不管多么异常的个人行为都更能引起惊讶。
Yet that is a rather trivial aspect of the matter. ->> 这只是问题的一个次要的方面。
The fact of first-rate importance is the predominant role that custom plays in experience and in belief, and the very great varieties it may manifest. ->> 最重要的是，风俗在经验和信仰中所起的主导作用，以及它所表现出来的极其丰富多样的形式。
No man ever looks at the world with pristine eyes. ->> 没有一个人是用纯洁无偏见的眼光看待世界的。
He sees it edited by a definite set of customs and institutions and ways of thinking. ->> 他所看到的是一个被特定风俗、制度和思想方式编辑过的世界。
Even in his philosophical probing he cannot go behind these stereotypes; his very concepts of the true and the false will still have reference to his particular traditional customs. ->> 甚至在哲学探索中，他也无法超越这些定型的框架；他关于真与假的概念仍与他特定的传统风俗有关。
John Dewey has said in all seriousness that the part played by custom in shaping the behaviour of the individual, as against any way in which he can affect traditional custom, is as the proportion of the total vocabulary of his mother tongue against those words of his own baby talk that are taken up into the vernacular of his family. ->> 约翰·杜威曾经非常严肃地指出：风俗在塑造个人行为中所起的作用与个人对风俗的影响相比，就如同他母语的总词汇量与他咿呀学语时被家庭接纳的那些词汇量之比。
When one seriously studies the social orders that have had the opportunity to develop autonomously, the figure becomes no more than an exact and matter-of-fact observation. ->> 当一个人认真研究那些有机会自主形成的社会秩序时，这种比喻无非就是一个准确且实事求是的观察。
The life history handed down in his community. ->> 个人的生活史是由其社群世代相传的生活方式和准则塑造的。
From the moment of his birth, the customs into which he is born shape his experience and behaviour. ->> 从他呱呱坠地的那一刻起，他生于其中的风俗便开始塑造他的经历和行为。
By the time he can talk, he is the little creature of his culture, and by the time he is grown and able to take part in its activities, its habits are his habits, its beliefs his beliefs, its impossibilities his impossibilities. ->> 到他会说话时，他就是文化塑造的一个小生物；等他长大了，能够参与活动时，这个文化的习惯就是他的习惯，这个文化的信仰就是他的信仰，这个文化所认为不可能的事情就是他所认为不可能的事情。
Every child that is born into his group will share them with him, and no child born into one on the opposite side of the globe can ever achieve the thousandth part. ->> 每一个和他诞生在同一个社群的孩子都会与他分享这些风俗，而地球另一端所诞生的孩子无法分享这种千分之一的经验。
There is no social problem it is more incumbent upon us to understand than this of the role of custom. ->> 没有任何社会问题比理解风俗的作用更为紧迫。
Until we are intelligent as to its laws and varieties, the main complicating facts of human life must remain unintelligible. ->> 在我们了解风俗的规律性和多样性之前，人类生活中主要复杂的事实仍然无法理解。
The study of custom can be profitable only after certain preliminary propositions have been accepted, and some of these propositions have been violently opposed. ->> 只有在某些基本主张被接受、同时有些主张被激烈反对后，风俗的研究才能有所裨益。
In the first place, any scientific study requires that there be no preferential weighting of one or another of the items in the series it selects for its consideration. ->> 首先，任何科学研究都要求对所选考虑的各因素不偏不倚。
In all the less controversial fields, like the study of cacti or termites or the nature of nebulae, the necessary method of study is to group the relevant material and to take note of all possible variant forms and conditions. ->> 在一些争议较小的领域，如对仙人掌、白蚁或星云性质的研究，研究方法应是将所有相关材料汇集，并注意所有可能的变异形式和条件。
In this way, we have learned all that we know of the laws of astronomy, or of the habits of the social insects. ->> 通过这种方法，我们掌握了所有关于天文学的规律和社会性昆虫习性的知识。
It is only in the study of man himself that the major social sciences have substituted the study of one local variation, that of Western civilization. ->> 仅在人类自身的研究中，主要的社会科学才以研究西方文明这一种局部变体来替代其他研究。
Anthropology was by definition impossible, as long as these distinctions between ourselves and the primitive, ourselves and the barbarian, ourselves and the pagan, held sway over people's minds. ->> 只要这些我们与原始人、野蛮人和异教徒之间的区别在人的思想中占据主导地位，按定义来说，人类学便是不可能的。
It was necessary first to arrive at that degree of sophistication where we no longer set our own belief against our neighbour's superstition. ->> 我们首先需要达到这样一种成熟程度：不再用自己的信仰去反对邻居的迷信。
It was necessary to recognize that these institutions which are based on the same premises, let us say the supernatural, must be considered together, our own among the rest. ->> 必须认识到，这些基于相同前提的制度（例如超自然观念），必须一视同仁并共同考虑，包括我们自己的制度。
