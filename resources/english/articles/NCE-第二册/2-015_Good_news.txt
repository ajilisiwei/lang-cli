The secretary told me that Mr Harmsworth would see me. ->> 秘书告诉我说哈姆斯沃斯先生要见我。
I felt very nervous when I went into his office. ->> 我走进他的办公室，感到非常紧张。
He did not look up from his desk when I entered. ->> 我进去的时候，他连头也没抬。
After I had sat down, he said that business was very bad. ->> 待我坐下后，他说生意非常不景气。
He told me that the firm could not afford to pay such large salaries. ->> 他还告诉我，公司支付不起这么庞大的工资开支。
Twenty people had already left. ->> 有20个人已经离去。
I knew that my turn had come. ->> 我知道这次该轮到我了。
'Mr Harmsworth,' I said in a weak voice. ->> “哈姆斯沃斯先生，”我无力地说。
'Don't interrupt,' he said. ->> “不要打断我的话，”他说。
Then he smiled and told me I would receive an extra £100 a year! ->> 然后他微笑了一下告诉我说，我每年将得到100英镑的额外收入。


secretary /ˈsɛkrəˌtɛri/ n. 秘书  
She became the secretary of the board last month. ->> 她上个月成为董事会的秘书。  
Everyone at the office depends on the efficiency of the secretary. ->> 办公室的每个人都依赖于秘书的效率。

nervous /ˈnərvəs/ adj. 精神紧张的  
He felt nervous before giving his presentation. ->> 他在做演讲前感到精神紧张。  
The nervous cat hid under the bed during the storm. ->> 精神紧张的猫在暴风雨中躲在床下。

afford /əˈfɔrd/ v. 负担得起  
Can you afford to buy a new car this year? ->> 你今年能负担得起买一辆新车吗？  
We couldn't afford the luxury vacation this time. ->> 这次我们负担不起豪华的假期。

weak /wik/ adj. 弱的  
After the illness, he felt too weak to get out of bed. ->> 生病后，他感到虚弱得无法下床。  
The weak light from the candle flickered in the wind. ->> 蜡烛发出的微弱光芒在风中摇曳。

interrupt /ˌɪntərˈəpt/ v. 插话，打断  
It’s rude to interrupt someone while they’re speaking. ->> 在别人说话时插话是不礼貌的。  
The loud noise from outside interrupted our conversation. ->> 外面的喧闹声打断了我们的谈话。
