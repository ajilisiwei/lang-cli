Appreciation of sculpture depends upon the ability to respond to form in three dimensions. ->> 对雕塑的鉴赏力取决于对三维形态的反应能力。
That is perhaps why sculpture has been described as the most difficult of all arts; certainly it is more difficult than the arts which involve appreciation of flat forms, shape in only two dimensions. ->> 雕塑被称为最难的艺术，可能就是这个原因。与只需欣赏二维平面形态的艺术相比，它当然更难。
Many more people are 'form-blind' than colour-blind. ->> “形盲”的人数比“色盲”的人数要多得多。
The child learning to see, first distinguishes only two-dimensional shape; it cannot judge distances, depths. ->> 正在学习看东西的儿童起初只能分辨二维形态，无法判断距离和深度。
Later, for its personal safety and practical needs, it has to develop (partly by means of touch) the ability to judge roughly three-dimensional distances. ->> 后来，由于自我安全和实际需要，儿童必须发展（部分通过触觉）粗略判断三维距离的能力。
But having satisfied the requirements of practical necessity, most people go no further. ->> 但在满足了实际需要后，大多数人就不再继续发展这种能力了。
Though they may attain considerable accuracy in the perception of flat form, they do not make the further intellectual and emotional effort needed to comprehend form in its full spatial existence. ->> 虽然他们对二维形态的感知可以达到相当准确的程度，但他们没有在智力和情感上进一步努力去理解形态在整个空间的存在。
This is what the sculptor must do. ->> 而雕塑家必须做到这一点。
He must strive continually to think of, and use, form in its full spatial completeness. ->> 他必须不断努力，从完整的空间角度思考和运用形态。
He gets the solid shape, as it were, inside his head-he thinks of it, whatever its size, as if he were holding it completely enclosed in the hollow of his hand. ->> 可以说，当他想象一个物体时，不论其大小，他脑中形成的是一个立体概念，就好像完全握在自己手心里一样。
He mentally visualizes a complex form from all round itself; he knows while he looks at one side what the other side is like, he identifies himself with its center of gravity, its mass, its weight; he realizes its volume, as the space that the shape displaces in the air. ->> 他在脑海中从各个角度勾画出物体的复杂形态，观看物体一侧时，他就已经知道另一侧的样子，他把自己与物体的重心、质量、重量融为一体，并意识到它的体积就是它在空气中所占的空间。
And the sensitive observer of sculpture must also learn to feel shape simply as shape, not as description or reminiscence. ->> 因此，敏锐的雕塑观赏者也必须学会把形态单纯地作为形态来感受，而不是通过描述或回忆来感受。
He must, for example, perceive an egg as a simple single solid shape, quite apart from its significance as food, or from the literary idea that it will become a bird. ->> 以鸟蛋为例，观赏者必须把它看作一个单一的实体形态，而完全不从它的食用意义或它会变成鸟这样的文学概念去感受。
And so with solids such as a shell, a nut, a plum, a pear, a tadpole, a mushroom, a mountain peak, a kidney, a carrot, a tree-trunk, a bird, a bud, a lark, a ladybird, a bulrush, a bone. ->> 对于其他实体，如贝壳、坚果、李子、梨子、蝌蚪、蘑菇、山顶、肾脏、胡萝卜、树干、鸟、花蕾、云雀、瓢虫、芦苇以及骨头，也应这样来感觉。
From these he can go on to appreciate more complex forms of combinations of several forms. ->> 从这些开始，他可以继续欣赏由多个形态组合而成的更复杂的形态。
