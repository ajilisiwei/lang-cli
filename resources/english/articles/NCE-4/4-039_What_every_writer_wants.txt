I have known very few writers, but those I have known, and whom I respected, confess at once that they have little idea where they are going when they first set pen to paper. ->> 我认识的作家寥寥无几，但是凡是我认识并尊敬的作家，都立即承认在他们动笔时，不清楚要写什么。

They have a character, perhaps two, they are in that condition of eager discomfort which passes for inspiration, all admit radical changes of destination once the journey has begun; one, to my certain knowledge, spent nine months on a novel about Kashmir, then reset the whole thing in the Scottish Highlands. ->> 他们心中可能有一两个角色，处在一种被当作灵感的急切不安状态中。一旦动笔，目的地的变化是剧烈的。据我所知，有位作家花了九个月写一部关于克什米尔的小说，后来却把整个故事背景换成了苏格兰高地。

I never heard of anyone making a 'skeleton', as we were taught at school. ->> 我从未听说有作家像我们在学校学的那样，动笔前先列提纲。

In the breaking and remaking, in the timing, interweaving, beginning afresh, the writer comes to discern things in his material which were not consciously in his mind when he began. ->> 作家在剪裁修改、构思时间、穿插情节、甚至从头重写的过程中，会领悟到素材中很多在动笔时未曾意识到的东西。

This organic process, often leading to moments of extraordinary self-discovery, is of an indescribable fascination. ->> 这种有机的加工过程往往带来不寻常的自我发现，具有难以言表的吸引力。

A blurred image appears, he adds a brushstroke and another, and it is gone; but something was there, and he will not rest till he has captured it. ->> 一个朦胧的形象出现在作家的脑海里，他左添一笔，右添一笔，形象反而消逝了；可是，好像有什么东西在那里，不把它捕捉到，作家是不会罢休的。

Sometimes the yeast within a writer outlives a book he has written. ->> 有时，作家完成一本书时，他的激情仍延续不止。

I have heard of writers who read nothing but their own books, like adolescents they stand before the mirror, and still cannot fathom the exact outline of the vision before them. ->> 我听说有些作家除了自己的书外什么都不读，他们就像青春期少年站在镜前，却仍然无法识别镜中影像的真实轮廓。

For the same reason, writers talk interminably about their own books, winkling out hidden meanings, super-imposing new ones, begging response from those around them. ->> 出于同样的原因，作家喋喋不休地谈论自己的书，挖掘隐晦的含义，叠加新的含义，恳求周围人的回应。

Of course a writer doing this is misunderstood: he might as well try to explain a crime or a love affair. ->> 作家这样做当然会被误解：他还不如尝试解释一个罪案或恋情。

He is also, incidentally, an unforgivable bore. ->> 顺便说一句，他也是个不可原谅的令人厌烦的人。

This temptation to cover the distance between himself and the reader, to study his image in the sight of those who do not know him, can be his undoing: he has begun to write to please. ->> 这种试图消除自己与读者之间的距离，试图从不了解自己的人眼中研究自己形象的诱惑可能会导致他的毁灭：因为他已经开始为取悦他人而写作。

A young English writer made the pertinent observation a year or two back that the talent goes into the first draft, and the art into the drafts that follow. ->> 一两年前，一位年轻的英国作家发表了中肯的看法：初稿体现才华，后续各稿体现艺术。

For this reason also the writer, like any other artist, has no resting place, no crowd or movement in which he may take comfort, no judgment from outside which can replace the judgment from within. ->> 也是由于这个原因，作家和其他艺术家一样，没有休息的地方，没有安慰自己的群体或活动，任何外界的判断都无法取代内心的判断。

A writer makes order out of the anarchy of his heart; he submits himself to a more ruthless discipline than any critic dreamed of, and when he flirts with fame, he is taking time off from living with himself, from the search for what his world contains at its inmost point. ->> 作家从内心的混乱中理出头绪，并将自己置于一种比任何批评家想象的都更无情的自律之下；当他玩弄名声时，他便在逃避与自我生活的相处，逃避探寻自己内心深处世界的真正内涵。
