We have been brought up to fear insects. ->> 我们自幼就在对昆虫的惧怕中长大。  
We regard them as unnecessary creatures that do more harm than good. ->> 我们把昆虫当作害多益少的无用之物。  
We continually wage war on them, for they contaminate our food, carry diseases, or devour our crops. ->> 我们不断与昆虫斗争，因为它们弄脏我们的食物，传播疾病，吞噬庄稼。  
They sting or bite without provocation; they fly uninvited into our rooms on summer nights, or beat against our lighted windows. ->> 它们无缘无故地又叮又咬；夏天的晚上，它们未经邀请便飞进我们的房间，或者对着亮着灯的窗户乱扑乱撞。  
We live in dread not only of unpleasant insects like spiders or wasps, but of quite harmless ones like moths. ->> 我们在日常生活中，不仅害怕如蜘蛛、黄蜂之类令人讨厌的昆虫，还害怕如飞蛾等无害的昆虫。  
Reading about them increases our understanding without dispelling our fears. ->> 阅读有关昆虫的书能加深我们对它们的了解，却不能消除我们的恐惧。  
Knowing that the industrious ant lives in a highly organized society does nothing to prevent us from being filled with revulsion when we find hordes of them crawling over a carefully prepared picnic lunch. ->> 即使知道勤劳的蚂蚁生活在一个高度组织化的社会里，当看到大群蚂蚁在我们精心准备的野餐上爬行时，我们仍然无法抑制对它们的反感。  
No matter how much we like honey, or how much we have read about the uncanny sense of direction which bees possess, we have a horror of being stung. ->> 不管我们多么爱吃蜂蜜，或读过多少关于蜜蜂神奇方向感的书，我们仍然十分害怕被蜂蜇。  
Most of our fears are unreasonable, but they are impossible to erase. ->> 我们的恐惧大部分是没有道理的，但却无法消除。  
At the same time, however, insects are strangely fascinating. ->> 然而，同时昆虫又有一种奇特的吸引力。  
We enjoy reading about them, especially when we find that, like the praying mantis, they lead perfectly horrible lives. ->> 我们喜欢读有关昆虫的书，尤其是当我们发现像螳螂这样的昆虫过着极其可怕的生活时。  
We enjoy staring at them, entranced as they go about their business, unaware (we hope) of our presence. ->> 我们喜欢入迷地看它们忙碌地做事，而它们（希望如此）不知道我们的存在。  
Who has not stood in awe at the sight of a spider pouncing on a fly, or a column of ants triumphantly bearing home an enormous dead beetle? ->> 当看到蜘蛛扑向苍蝇，或一队蚂蚁凯旋地抬着一只巨大的死甲虫回家时，谁能不感到敬畏呢？  
Last summer I spent days in the garden watching thousands of ants crawling up the trunk of my prize peach tree. ->> 去年夏天，我花了好几天时间在花园里观察成千上万的蚂蚁爬上我那棵心爱的桃树的树干。  
The tree has grown against a warm wall on a sheltered side of the house. ->> 那棵树靠着房子有遮挡的一面暖墙生长。  
I am especially proud of it, not only because it has survived several severe winters, but because it occasionally produces luscious peaches. ->> 我对这棵树感到特别自豪，不仅因为它度过了几个严冬，而且因为它有时候会结出甜美的桃子。  
During the summer, I noticed that the leaves of the tree were beginning to wither. ->> 到了夏天，我注意到树叶开始枯萎。  
Clusters of tiny insects called aphids were to be found on the underside of the leaves. ->> 在树叶底下可以找到成群的微小昆虫，即蚜虫。  
They were visited by a large colony of ants which obtained a sort of honey from them. ->> 它们被一大群蚂蚁拜访，这些蚂蚁从它们那里获取一种类似蜂蜜的物质。  
I immediately embarked on an experiment which, even though it failed to get rid of the ants, kept me fascinated for twenty-four hours. ->> 我立刻进行了一项实验，尽管没有能够消灭蚂蚁，却让我整整24小时沉迷其中。  
I bound the base of the tree with sticky tape, making it impossible for the ants to reach the aphids. ->> 我用胶带把桃树底部缠住，使蚂蚁无法接近蚜虫。  
The tape was so sticky that they did not dare to cross it. ->> 胶带非常粘，蚂蚁不敢从上面爬过。  
For a long time, I watched them scurrying around the base of the tree in bewilderment. ->> 在很长一段时间里，我看着蚂蚁在树底部迷惑地转来转去。  
I even went out at midnight with a torch and noted with satisfaction (and surprise) that the ants were still swarming around the sticky tape without being able to do anything about it. ->> 我甚至在午夜带着手电筒去看看，满意地（也惊讶地）发现蚂蚁仍然在胶带周围团团转，却无能为力。  
I got up early next morning hoping to find that the ants had given up in despair. ->> 第二天一早我起床，希望看到蚂蚁因为绝望而放弃。  
Instead, I saw that they had discovered a new route. ->> 结果却发现它们找到了新的路径。  
They were climbing up the wall of the house and then on to the leaves of the tree. ->> 它们正在顺着房子的墙爬上去，然后再爬到树叶上。  
I realized sadly that I had been completely defeated by their ingenuity. ->> 我悲哀地意识到，我已经完全败在了蚂蚁的聪明才智下。  
The ants had been quick to find an answer to my thoroughly unscientific methods! ->> 蚂蚁很快就找到了应对我这种完全不科学方法的办法！
