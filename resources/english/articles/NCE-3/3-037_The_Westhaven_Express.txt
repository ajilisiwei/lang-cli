We have learnt to expect that trains will be punctual. ->> 我们已经习惯于相信火车总是准点的。  
After years of conditioning, most of us have developed an unshakable faith in railway timetables. ->> 经过多年的适应，大多数人对火车时刻表产生了一种不可动摇的信念。  
Ships may be delayed by storms; flights may be cancelled because of bad weather, but trains must be on time. ->> 轮船船期可能因风暴而推迟，飞机航班可能因恶劣天气而取消，但火车必须准时。  
Only an exceptionally heavy snowfall might temporarily dislocate railway services. ->> 只有非同寻常的大雪才可能暂时扰乱铁路服务。  
It is all too easy to blame the railway authorities when something does go wrong. ->> 因此，一旦铁路上真出了问题，人们便很容易责备铁路当局。  
The truth is that when mistakes occur, they are more likely to be ours than theirs. ->> 事实上，当出现错误时，很可能是我们的失误，而不是铁路当局的。  
After consulting my railway timetable, I noted with satisfaction that there was an express train to Westhaven. ->> 我查看了列车时刻表，满意地发现有一趟去威斯特海温的快车。  
It went direct from my local station and the journey lasted mere hour and seventeen minutes. ->> 这是趟直达车，旅程仅需1小时17分钟。  
When I boarded the train, I could not help noticing that a great many local people got on as well. ->> 上车后，我注意到许多当地人也上了车。  
At the time, this did not strike me as odd. ->> 当时，我并不感到奇怪。  
I reflected that there must be a great many local people besides myself who wished to take advantage of this excellent service. ->> 我想，除了我之外，肯定还有许多当地人也想利用这项优质服务。  
Neither was I surprised when the train stopped at Widley, a tiny station a few miles along the line. ->> 当火车在几英里外的一个小站威德里停下来时，我也不感到惊讶。  
Even a mighty express train can be held up by signals. ->> 即便是高速快车也可能被信号拦住。  
But when the train dawdled at station after station, I began to wonder. ->> 但是，当火车一站接着一站地缓慢移动时，我开始怀疑。  
It suddenly dawned on me that this express was not roaring down the line at ninety miles an hour, but barely chugging along at thirty. ->> 我突然意识到这趟快车并没有以每小时90英里的速度飞驰，而只是以每小时30英里的速度缓慢前行。  
One hour and seventeen minutes passed and we had not even covered half the distance. ->> 1小时17分钟过去了，我们还没有走完一半的路程。  
I asked a passenger if this was the Westhaven Express, but he had not even heard of it. ->> 我问一位乘客这是不是开往威斯特海温的快车，但他从未听说过有这么一趟快车。  
I determined to lodge a complaint as soon as we arrived. ->> 我决定一到目的地就投诉铁路部门。  
Two hours later, I was talking angrily to the station master at Westhaven. ->> 两小时后，我愤怒地对威斯特海温站的站长说起此事。  
When he denied the train's existence, I borrowed his copy of the timetable. ->> 当他否认有这趟列车时，我借了他的列车时刻表。  
There was a note of triumph in my voice when I told him that it was there in black and white. ->> 当我告诉他时刻表上白纸黑字印着这趟列车时，我的语气中带着胜利感。  
Glancing at it briefly, he told me to look again. ->> 他迅速地扫了一眼，然后让我再看一遍。  
A tiny asterisk conducted me to a footnote at the bottom of the page. ->> 一个小小的星号将我的目光引到了那页底部的一个注释上。  
It said: 'This service has been suspended.' ->> 上面写着：“此趟列车暂停运行。”
