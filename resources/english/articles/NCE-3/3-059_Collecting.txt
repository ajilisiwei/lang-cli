People tend to amass possessions, sometimes without being aware of doing so. ->> 人们倾向于积累物品，有时甚至没有意识到自己在这样做。

Indeed they can have a delightful surprise when they find something useful which they did not know they owned. ->> 确实，当他们发现某件有用的东西而不知道自己拥有它时，会感到惊喜。

Those who never have to move house become indiscriminate collectors of what can only be described as clutter. ->> 那些从来不需要搬家的人成了杂物的随意收藏者。

They leave unwanted objects in drawers, cupboards and attics for years, in the belief that they may one day need just those very things. ->> 他们将不需要的东西放在抽屉、橱柜和阁楼里，一放就是好几年，相信总有一天会用到它们。

As they grow old, people also accumulate belongings for two other reasons, lack of physical and mental energy, both of which are essential in turning out and throwing away, and sentiment. ->> 人们年老之后也因两个原因积累物品：一是缺乏清理和丢弃它们所需的体力和精力，另一是出于情感。

Things owned for a long time are full associations with the past, perhaps with relatives who are dead, and so they gradually acquire a value beyond their true worth. ->> 长期拥有的东西充满着与过去的联系，可能与已故的亲戚有关，因此它们逐渐获得了超出其本身价值的意义。

Some things are collected deliberately in the home in an attempt to avoid waste. ->> 居家时有意地收藏某些东西是为了避免浪费。

Among these I would list string and brown paper, kept by thrifty people when a parcel has been opened, to save buying these two requisites. ->> 在这些物品中，我想提到线绳和包装纸，节俭的人们在打开包裹后会把它们留下，以免日后购买。

Collecting small items can easily become a mania. ->> 收藏小物件很容易变成一种癖好。

I know someone who always cuts sketches out from newspapers of model clothes that she would like to buy if she had the money. ->> 我认识一位总是从报纸上剪下她希望买的流行服装图样的女士，如果她有钱的话。

As she is not rich, the chances that she will ever be able to afford such purchases are remote; but she is never sufficiently strong-minded to be able to stop the practice. ->> 由于她并不富裕，她买得起这些衣服的可能性很小，但她总是缺乏足够坚强的意志去停止这种做法。

It is a harmless habit, but it litters up her desk to such an extent that every time she opens it, loose bits of paper fall out in every direction. ->> 这是一种无害的习惯，但桌子的凌乱程度让她每次打开都会有纸屑到处散落。

Collecting as a serious hobby is quite different and has many advantages. ->> 作为一种严肃业余爱好的收藏活动完全不同，并有许多益处。

It provides relaxation for leisure hours, as just looking at one's treasures is always a joy. ->> 它在闲暇时提供放松，因为观赏自己的珍藏总能带来快乐。

One does not have to go outside for amusement, since the collection is housed at home. ->> 不需要外出寻求娱乐，因为收藏品就放在家中。

Whatever it consists of, stamps, records, first editions of books, china, glass, antique furniture, pictures, model cars, stuffed birds, toy animals, there is always something to do in connection with it, from finding the right place for the latest addition to verifying facts in reference books. ->> 无论是邮票、唱片、初版书籍、瓷器、玻璃、古董家具、图片、模型汽车、鸟类标本还是玩具动物，总有事情可做，从为新添收藏品找合适的位置到在参考书中核实信息。

This hobby educates one not only in the chosen subject but also in general matters which have some bearing on it. ->> 这种爱好不仅能让人在所选择的主题中受益，还能在与之相关的一般事项上获得提升。

There are also other benefits. ->> 还有其他好处。

One wants to meet like-minded collectors, to get advice, to compare notes, to exchange articles, to show off the latest find. ->> 人们会见志趣相投的收藏者，以获取建议、交换经验、分享收藏品和展示最新发现。

So one's circle of friends grows. ->> 于是他们的朋友圈不断扩大。

Soon the hobby leads to travel, perhaps to a meeting in another town, possibly a trip abroad in search of a rare specimen, for collectors are not confined to any one country. ->> 很快，这种爱好就会带来旅行，可能去另一个城市参加会议，甚至出国寻找稀有标本，因为收藏家并不限于某一个国家。

Over the years, one may well become an authority on one's hobby and will very probably be asked to give informal talks to little gatherings and then, if successful, to larger audiences. ->> 经过多年的积累，一个人可能会成为他所热衷的爱好的权威，而且很可能受到邀请在小型聚会上做非正式演讲，如果成功的话，还可能面向更多听众发表演说。

In this way self-confidence grows, first from mastering a subject, then from being able to talk about it. ->> 通过这种方式，自信心不断增强，首先因为掌握了一门学问，然后是因为能够就此发表见解。

Collecting, by occupying spare time so constructively, makes a person contented, with no time for boredom. ->> 收藏，通过如此有建设性地占用空闲时间，让人感到满足，没有时间感到无聊。
